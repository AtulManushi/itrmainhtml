Address Amendment Within state (LLP)

When a Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) changes its registered office address within the same state, it must comply with the provisions laid down under the LLP Act, 2008. The LLP must file Form 15 with the Registrar of Companies (ROC) within 30 days of the address change, providing details of the new registered office along with a copy of the LLP agreement (if it reflects the change), board resolution or consent of all partners, and proof of the new address (such as a utility bill or rental agreement). This ensures that the official records of the LLP remain accurate and up to date.

A Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) may need to change its registered office address within the same state for various reasons such as operational convenience, business expansion, or cost efficiency. As per the LLP Act, 2008, any such change must be officially recorded with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) to ensure that the Registrar of Companies (ROC) has up-to-date information about the LLP‚Äôs registered office. This registered address serves as the primary location for receiving legal and governmental communications.

The required filings, including Form 15 to notify the Registrar of Companies (ROC) about the change, have been submitted with all necessary documents such as the consent of partners, proof of the new office address, and the amended LLP agreement reflecting the new address.

To initiate the change, the LLP must file Form 15 with the ROC within 30 days from the date of the decision to shift the office. The form must be accompanied by necessary documents including proof of the new address (e.g., a utility bill, lease deed, or ownership document), consent of all partners (or resolution passed as per the LLP agreement), and a copy of the updated LLP agreement if it includes the new address. It is important that the address change is approved internally by all partners in accordance with the procedures laid out in the LLP agreement.

Once filed and accepted by the ROC, the new address becomes the official registered office of the LLP. This means all government notices, tax correspondence, and legal documents will be directed to the new location. It is critical that this address be accessible and functional, as any failure to receive or respond to official communications could lead to legal or compliance issues for the LLP.

Failure to notify the ROC within the prescribed time frame may result in penalties and may affect the LLP's compliance status. Moreover, financial institutions and other stakeholders often verify the LLP's registered address with the MCA portal, so an outdated address could cause confusion or delays in business transactions. Therefore, timely and accurate filing of address amendments is not just a legal requirement but also a best practice for maintaining transparency and business continuity.
