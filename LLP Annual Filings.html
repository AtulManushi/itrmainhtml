Each financial year, an LLP must file two key statements with the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA): Form 11 (Annual Return) and Form 8 (Statement of Account and Solvency). Form 11, which contains details of the partners and any changes during the year, must be filed by 30th May of each year. Form 8, which includes the LLP‚Äôs financial statements and declaration of solvency, is due by 30th October of each year.

Additionally, LLPs that exceed prescribed turnover limits are required to get their accounts audited by a Chartered Accountant. These filings ensure transparency and legal standing, helping LLPs maintain good standing with regulatory authorities and financial institutions.

Form 11 is a relatively simple form that provides an overview of the LLP‚Äôs basic structure, including details of partners and designated partners, contribution received, and any changes in management. On the other hand, Form 8 is more comprehensive and includes the LLP‚Äôs balance sheet, profit and loss statement, and a declaration regarding the solvency of the business.
